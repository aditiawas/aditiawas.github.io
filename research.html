Research

My work sits at the intersection of quantum computing architecture, compilation, and error correction — with the goal of making fault-tolerant quantum computers practical.

Baker Lab

The University of Texas at Austin

Aug 2024 – Present

Conducting research in quantum computing architecture and error correction geared towards developing robust quantum circuit compilation and optimization techniques.

Quantum Resource Estimation for FTQC

Modeled magic-state factory allocation for fault-tolerant quantum circuits, and characterized space–time trade-offs across distillation, cultivation, and Rz-synthesis regimes under realistic error models.

Pipelining and Speculation for FTQC

Designed and analyzed speculative execution and load-balancing strategies for quantum circuits.

Trade-offs in Decoding Algorithms for Surface Codes ↗ GitHub

Analyzed Sparse Blossom decoder performance under limited parallelization and partitioning constraints, identifying optimization strategies for latency vs. accuracy trade-offs.
